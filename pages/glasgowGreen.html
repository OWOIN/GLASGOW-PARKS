Glasgow Green

Glasgow Green is on the banks of the Clyde, to the east of the city centre. It is the oldest official park in the city and was established around the 15th century. The Park itself is about 136 acres in size and was awarded the “Green Flag award” which is the national benchmark for green spaces and parks.

Glasgow Green is a large park with many enterances, but is most easily accessable via a 5 minute walk for Argyle Street train station.

The park has no gates so is never officially shut, However due to its size, much of the park is not lit up at night so going after dark is not recommended.

  • Winter Gardens: 10am - 5pm
  • West Brewery: 11am - 12am

The park was officially given to the people of Glasgow in 1450 by King James II. It took about 100 years for the marshland from the side of the clyde to be cleared and the park became more recognisable to what we see today.

Nelson's Monument was constructed in 1806 and the St Andrews Bridge was built in 1855 to allow an easy crossing over the river from within the park. The Peoples Palace and fountain were built around 1881.
